Hua Cui, Professor of Analytical Chemistry

Hua Cui received her Ph. D. in Analytical Chemistry from University of Science and Technology of China (USTC)  in 1990. In 2000, she received the financial support from "Hundred Talents Program" of Chinese Academy of Sciences, and is now a professor of analytical chemistry at the Department of Chemistry, University of Science and Technology of China, Hefei, P.R. China.  (contact to Prof. Hua Cui >>>)

　
Research Interests

The research fields include the cross-subjects of chemiluminescence (CL) and electrochemistry, chromatography, nano science, and their applications in food safety determination, clinical medicine, and the monitoring of environment. The current interests are as follow:

(1) Potential-resolved electrogenerated chemiluminescence
(2) Chemiluminescence of the flow system
(3) Liquid phase chemiluminescence and electrogenerated chemiluminescence involved by nanoparticles

Main contribution

    Present the Potential-Resolved Electrohemiluminescence (PRECL), which means the multi-reaction-channels of traditional chemiluminescent reagents at different work potentials, and the concept of multi-channel emission in ECL. The great dependence between the different ECL channel and the electrode potential, material of electrode and the surface statements of electrode are studied and fully discussed. Based on these results, the ECL mechanism of different channels were proposed.

    The enhancement and inhibition rules of CL and ECL of a series of phenolic and aniline compounds, and the dependence between the light emission and the molecular structures, concentrations, and the solution pH, were concluded, and the mechanism of these phenomenon were also proposed. And then, the corresponding flow-injection with CL detection method and the HPLC-CL method of these compounds were established. Furthermore, we discovered a new CL system, i.e., Ce (IV) + Tween 20, proposed the reaction mechanism, and established the high sensitivity CL analysis to many phenolic and flavonic compounds.

  It was found that the ECL behaviors of luminol on gold nanoparticle self-assembled electrodes were much different from those on conventional electrodes. The light intensity was greatly enhanced; new emission channels appeared; and stability and reproducibility were largely improved. The mechanisms of each ECL channel were aslo discussed. Furthermore, it has revealed that gold nanoparticles can be used as energy acceptor, reductant, nsnosized reaction platform or catalyst to induce liquid phase chemiluminescence, and their reaction mechanisms were discussed as well.
